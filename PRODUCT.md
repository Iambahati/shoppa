# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Shoppa serves two primary user groups:
- **Shoppers** looking to browse, purchase, and track orders from local vendors.
- **Vendors** managing their store, inventory, and orders.

Both groups encounter the auth screens (login/register) as the entry point to the product. Their context is task-driven: they want to get into the marketplace quickly, but also feel trust and warmth from the brand.

## Product Purpose

Shoppa is a marketplace platform that connects vendors and shoppers. It enables vendors to list products and manage their business, while shoppers discover and buy from trusted local sellers. Success means frictionless commerce with a human, community-oriented feel.

## Brand Personality

- Warm
- Welcoming
- Approachable

Voice is friendly and clear, never corporate or cold. The interface should feel like a trusted local market moved online: personal, reliable, and easy to navigate.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS template aesthetic (cold grays, sharp corners, sterile layouts)
- Overly corporate fintech / enterprise dashboards
- High-contrast dark-mode tool interfaces
- Cluttered marketplace UIs with aggressive upselling

## Design Principles

1. **Friendly over formal**: Rounded forms, soft colors, and approachable typography lower the barrier to entry.
2. **Clarity over cleverness**: Auth flows should be obvious; no hidden actions or ambiguous labels.
3. **Warmth through restraint**: Subtle sky-blue and blush accents on a clean canvas, not overwhelming color.
4. **Balance for all users**: Returning and new users are given equal visual weight on auth surfaces.
5. **Trust through craft**: Polished details (spacing, focus states, responsive behavior) signal a product that cares about its users.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations.
- Ensure form fields have clear labels, error messages, and focus indicators.
- Touch targets at least 44×44 px.
